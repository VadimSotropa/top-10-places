Barcelona

Barcelona, located on the northeastern coast of Spain, is a vibrant and dynamic city known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning architecture. As the capital of Catalonia, Barcelona is home to iconic works by the renowned architect Antoni Gaudí such as La Sagrada Familia, Park Guell, and Casa Batllo.

Visitors can explore the Gothic Quarter, a historic neighborhood filled with medieval architecture and narrow streets. Art lovers can also visit the Picasso Museum and Joan Miró Foundation for a glimpse into the works of these famous artists. The city also offers a diverse range of activities such as Montserrat and its beautiful mountain range, the Magic Fountain, and Barceloneta Beach.

Barcelona is also famous for its lively atmosphere and nightlife. The city offers a diverse culinary scene and shopping is also an experience with a wide variety of independent boutiques, local markets, and famous brands. Also, Football fans can't miss the visit of FC Barcelona's stadium, Camp Nou. Barcelona is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a mix of culture, history, entertainment, and the best of Spanish art and architecture.

1. La Sagrada Familia

La Sagrada Familia is one of the most iconic and well-known landmarks in Barcelona. This basilica, designed by the famous architect Antoni Gaudí, is still under construction and is set to be completed in 2026. It's a must-see attraction for anyone visiting the city. But did you know that La Sagrada Familia has some interesting facts that you might not have heard before?


  • Construction of La Sagrada Familia began in 1882 and is expected to be completed in 2026, making it one of the longest running construction projects in history.

  • The basilica's famous towers are inspired by the Nativity Façade and the Passion Façade, respectively.

  • It is said that Gaudí was so dedicated to the project that he even moved into an apartment on the site, which is now a museum dedicated to his life and works.
