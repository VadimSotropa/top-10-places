Dubai

Dubai is a vibrant city known for its luxury and modernity. The city boasts iconic landmarks such as the Burj Khalifa, the world's tallest building, and the Palm Jumeirah, a man-made island shaped like a palm tree.

Dubai offers a wide range of activities from shopping at the renowned Dubai Mall to skiing at the indoor ski resort, Ski Dubai. The city is also known for its gastronomy, with a diverse selection of restaurants serving international cuisine.

Dubai is a perfect destination for families, luxury travellers, and adventure seekers. Explore the city's traditional souks or take a desert safari for a truly unique experience.

1. Burj Al Arab Jumeirah

Burj Al Arab Jumeirah is one of the world's most iconic and luxurious hotels. The hotel's distinctive sail-shaped silhouette and unparalleled amenities have made it a symbol of opulence and extravagance. Here are some interesting facts about Burj Al Arab Jumeirah that you might not have heard before:


  • Burj Al Arab Jumeirah is the only 7-star hotel in the world. The hotel's exceptional level of luxury and service has earned it this unique distinction.

  • The hotel's atrium is 180 meters tall, making it one of the tallest in the world.

  • Burj Al Arab Jumeirah has a private beach, a fleet of Rolls Royce cars and even a helicopter landing pad.

  • The hotel's Royal Suite, which is the most expensive in the hotel, cost around $24,000 per night.

  • The hotel's architecture is inspired by the traditional Arabian dhow, an ancient sailing vessel.

2. Jumeirah Emirates Tower

Jumeirah Emirates Towers is one of the city's most iconic and recognizable skyscrapers. The towers, which consist of the Jumeirah Emirates Tower One and Two, stand out for their sleek and modern design, as well as their prime location in the heart of the city's financial district. Here are some interesting facts about Jumeirah Emirates Towers that you might not have heard before:


  • Jumeirah Emirates Tower One is 355 meters tall, making it one of the tallest buildings in the city.

  • Tower Two stands at 309 meters, and largely contains offices across its 54 floors.

  • The towers were designed by the renowned architecture firm NORR Group, which is well-known for its work on skyscrapers and high-rise buildings.

  • The towers are connected by a multi-level shopping complex, known as The Boulevard, which features luxury boutiques, fine dining restaurants and a variety of other amenities.

3. Burj Khalifa

Burj Khalifa is the tallest man-made structure in the world, standing at 828 meters (2,722 feet). The tower's design and construction were a true engineering marvel, and it has become an iconic symbol of Dubai's ambition and rapid development. Here are some interesting facts about Burj Khalifa that you might not have heard before:


  • Burj Khalifa took over six years to construct and was completed in 2010.

  • Over 330,000 cubic meters of concrete, 39,000 tons of steel rebar, and 142,000 square meters of glass were used in the construction of the tower.

  • Burj Khalifa has 163 floors, with the highest occupied floor being the 148th.

  • The tower's observation deck, At The Top, is located on the 124th floor and offers breathtaking views of the city.

  • Burj Khalifa has 57 elevators, including the world's fastest double-deck elevators, which can travel at speeds of up to 22 meters per second.

4. Desert safari

5. The Palm Jumeirah

The Palm Jumeirah is an artificial archipelago shaped in the form of a palm tree. It was created using land reclamation and is one of the three Palm Islands, the other two being The Palm Jebel Ali and The Palm Deira. Here are some interesting facts about The Palm Jumeirah that you might not have heard before:


  • The Palm Jumeirah was first proposed by His Highness Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, the ruler of Dubai, in 2001.

  • The Palm Jumeirah is home to a variety of luxury resorts, hotels, villas, apartments and beaches.

  • The Palm Jumeirah is the smallest of the three Palm Islands and is the only one that is currently open to the public.

  • The Palm Jumeirah is approximately 5 km by 5 km and is connected to the mainland by a 300-meter bridge.

  • The Palm Jumeirah is visible from space and is considered to be one of the world's eight wonders of the modern world.

6. The Fountains

The Fountains of Dubai is a choreographed fountain system that is set on the 30-acre man-made Burj Khalifa Lake, at the center of the Downtown Dubai development. It was designed by the creators of the Fountains of Bellagio in Las Vegas. Here are some interesting facts about The Fountain of Dubai that you might not have heard before:


  • The Fountains of Dubai is the world's largest choreographed fountain system and is visible from space.

  • The fountains features a range of water jets and lights that are synchronized to music and can shoot water as high as 150 meters (492 feet) in the air.

  • The fountains has five circles of varying sizes and two central arcs, which can shoot water in different directions and heights.

  • The fountain's water jets are powered by a network of pumps that can pump water at a rate of up to 22,000 gallons per second.

  • The fountains has a total of 6,600 lights and 25 color projectors that are used to create a variety of different lighting effects.

7. Dubai Creek

Dubai Creek is a historic saltwater inlet that runs 6 miles through the city and is considered to be the birthplace of modern Dubai. It has played a significant role in the city's economic and cultural development over the centuries. Here are some interesting facts about Dubai Creek that you might not have heard before:


  • Dubai Creek is a natural inlet of the Gulf and separates the city into two main areas: Deira and Bur Dubai.

  • The creek was an important center of trade and commerce in the region since ancient times and was used as a port for ships carrying goods such as pearls, gold, and spices.

  • Dubai Creek was the site of the first permanent settlement in Dubai, and today it is home to several historic districts such as Al Shindagha, Al Fahidi, and Al Bastakiya.

  • Visitors can take an abra ride, a traditional wooden boat, to cross the creek and explore the surrounding areas.

  • Dubai Creek Park is a large park and recreational area that runs along the creek and offers a variety of activities such as fishing, picnicking, and bird-watching.

8. Dubai Museum

Dubai Museum is a museum that is housed in the Al Fahidi Fort, which is the oldest existing building in Dubai. The museum was opened in 1971 and provides an overview of the city's history, culture and development. Here are some interesting facts about Dubai Museum that you might not have heard before:


  • The Dubai Museum is located in the Al Fahidi Fort, which was built in 1787 to defend the city against invasion.

  • The museum's exhibits include dioramas, artifacts, and interactive displays that provide a glimpse into the city's past and its traditional way of life.

  • The museum's collection includes items such as traditional weapons, textiles, and costumes, as well as a section on the city's pearl diving industry.

  • The museum also features a section on the Bedouin lifestyle and desert life, including a reconstruction of a traditional Bedouin house.

  • The museum also holds temporary exhibits, showcasing the city's art and cultural heritage, as well as the latest archaeological discoveries in the region.

10. The Dubai Miracle Garden

The Dubai Miracle Garden, located in Dubailand, UAE, is the world's largest flower garden, featuring over 45 million flowers in a variety of shapes and colors. The garden is a popular tourist attraction, known for its stunning floral displays and unique landscaping. Here are some interesting facts about The Dubai Miracle Garden that you might not have heard before:


  • The Dubai Miracle Garden covers an area of 72,000 square meters, making it the world's largest flower garden.

  • The garden features over 45 million flowers of different varieties and colors, including roses, petunias, marigolds, and more.

  • The garden also features a variety of floral displays such as flower-shaped structures, floral arches, and topiary sculptures.

  • The garden also features a butterfly garden, where visitors can see a wide variety of butterflies in a natural habitat.

  • The Dubai Miracle Garden is open from October to April, and is closed during the summer months to protect the flowers from the heat.
