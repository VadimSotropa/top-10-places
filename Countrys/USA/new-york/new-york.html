New York

New York City is a vibrant and dynamic metropolis known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning architecture. As one of the most iconic cities in the world, New York is home to world-renowned landmarks such as the Statue of Liberty, Central Park, and the Empire State Building.

Visitors can explore the diverse neighborhoods, from the historic streets of Greenwich Village to the bustling energy of Times Square. Art lovers can also visit the Metropolitan Museum of Art and the Museum of Modern Art for a glimpse into the works of famous artists. The city also offers a diverse range of activities such as taking a ferry to the Statue of Liberty, strolling through Central Park, and enjoying the views from the top of the Empire State Building.

New York is also famous for its lively atmosphere and nightlife. The city offers a diverse culinary scene and shopping is also an experience with a wide variety of independent boutiques, local markets, and famous brands. New York is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a mix of culture, history, entertainment, and the best of American art and architecture.

1. Empire State Building

The Empire State Building is one of New York City's most famous landmarks and is known for its stunning views of the city. The building stands at a whopping 1,454 feet tall and was completed in 1931. But did you know that the Empire State Building has some interesting facts that you might not have heard before?


  • The Empire State Building was the tallest building in the world until 1970.

  • The building's observation deck is open until 2 a.m. on weekends, making it a popular spot for night views of the city.

  • The Empire State Building has been featured in numerous films and TV shows, including King Kong and Sleepless in Seattle.

  • The building is also said to have been the site of over 80 suicides since it opened.

2. Central Park

Central Park is one of the most famous and beloved public parks in the world. Located in the heart of Manhattan, it covers 843 acres and offers a wide variety of activities and attractions for visitors of all ages. But did you know that Central Park has some interesting facts that you might not have heard before?


  • Central Park was officially opened in 1857.

  • The park receives over 42 million visitors annually, making it the most visited urban park in the United States.

  • The park features over 26,000 trees, 9,000 benches, 21 playgrounds, and 36 bridges and arches.

  • A funny fact is that Central Park has its own Delacorte Theater, where it has been held the famous "Shakespeare in the Park" festival since 1962.

4. Metropolitan Museum of Art

The Metropolitan Museum of Art, also known as the Met, is one of the most famous and largest art museums in the United States.   • The museum's collection includes over 2 million works, including paintings, sculptures, decorative arts, and musical instruments.

5. Times Square

Times Square, located in the heart of Manhattan, New York City, is one of the most famous tourist destinations in the world. But did you know that Times Square has some interesting facts that you might not have heard before?

  • Times Square is known for its billboards and advertisements, with an estimated annual cost of $2.5 million for a single ad on one of the large digital billboards.

6. 9/11 Memorial & Museum

The 9/11 Memorial & Museum is a tribute to the victims and a place of remembrance for the tragic events that occurred on September 11, 2001. The memorial features two reflecting pools, each one acre in size, located in the footprints of the Twin Towers. The museum offers a unique perspective on the events of 9/11 through artifacts, stories and multimedia exhibits. But did you know that the 9/11 Memorial & Museum has some facts that you might not have heard before?


  • The memorial was officially opened to the public on September 11, 2011, the 10th anniversary of the attacks.

  • The museum receives over 3 million visitors annually, making it one of the most visited sites in New York City.

  • The museum's collection includes over 10,000 artifacts, including steel beams from the Twin Towers, the "Survivor Stairs" used by many to escape the towers, and personal items belonging to victims.

  • The museum also has a "Last Column" from the Twin Towers, which is covered in messages and signatures from recovery workers and visitors.

8. Top of The Rock

Top of the Rock is an observation deck located in the Rockefeller Center in New York City, offering panoramic views of the city from the 67th, 69th, and 70th floors. But did you know that Top of the Rock has some interesting facts that you might not have heard before?


  • Top of the Rock was officially opened in 1933.

  • The observatory deck is open 365 days a year, and visitors can see the city during the day or at night.

  • The observation deck is 250 feet above street level, offering a 360-degree view of the city.

  • The observation deck was closed for a few years during World War II, as the government believed it to be a potential target for enemy attacks.

10. Brooklyn Bridge

The Brooklyn Bridge is a historic landmark and one of the oldest suspension bridges in the United States. Connecting the boroughs of Manhattan and Brooklyn, the bridge spans the East River and offers incredible views of the city. But did you know that the Brooklyn Bridge has some interesting facts that you might not have heard before?


  • The Brooklyn Bridge was officially opened in 1883 and took 14 years to construct.

  • The bridge's main span is 1,595.5 feet, making it the longest suspension bridge span in the world at the time of its opening.

  • The Brooklyn Bridge was the first steel-wire suspension bridge ever built, and the first steel-wire suspension bridge to be built in the United States.

  • It's said that during the construction, people were skeptical of the new technology and called it "Roebling's Folly" after the chief engineer.
