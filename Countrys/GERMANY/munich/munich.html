Munich

Munich, the capital of the German state of Bavaria, is known for its rich history, culture, and iconic landmarks. As one of the most visited cities in Germany, Munich offers something for everyone to enjoy. Visitors can explore famous landmarks such as Marienplatz, the Frauenkirche and the Nymphenburg Palace.

The city also boasts beautiful parks such as the English Garden, and world-renowned museums like the Alte Pinakothek and the Deutsches Museum. Munich's culinary scene is diverse, with a wide variety of traditional German dishes and international cuisine. Especially, Oktoberfest is a must-visit event in Munich, where you can taste the traditional German beer and food.

Shopping in Munich is a unique experience, with a wide range of independent boutiques and famous department stores such as the Kaufhaus des Westens. Munich is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a mix of culture, history, entertainment, and the best of German art and architecture.

1. Marienplatz

Marienplatz is the central square of Munich and is known for its famous Glockenspiel, a clock tower with moving figures that perform twice daily. It's a lively place where you can find street performers, vendors and locals hanging out. But did you know that Marienplatz has some interesting facts that you might not have heard before?


  • The Glockenspiel performance has been delighting visitors since 1908.

  • The Marienplatz is surrounded by some of the most famous buildings in Munich, including the New Town Hall and the Old Town Hall.

  • In the past, the Marienplatz was used as a marketplace and a place for public executions.

  • The Marienplatz is also home to the famous Christmas market during the holiday season.

  • The Alte Pinakothek is one of the most visited art museums in Germany, attracting more than 600,000 visitors annually.

10. Oktoberfest

Oktoberfest is a famous annual festival held in Munich, Germany, celebrating Bavarian culture and beer. The festival attracts millions of visitors from around the world and is known for its traditional beer tents, delicious food, and lively atmosphere. But did you know that Oktoberfest has some interesting facts that you might not have heard before?


  • Oktoberfest was first held in 1810 to celebrate the marriage of Crown Prince Ludwig and Princess Therese of Saxe-Hildburghausen.

  • The festival is held annually in September and lasts for 16-18 days.

  • Oktoberfest is the world's largest fair, with over 6 million people attending each year.

  • Oktoberfest is not only about beer, visitors can also enjoy traditional Bavarian food, carnival rides, and live music.

  • Oktoberfest is a must-see destination for anyone interested in experiencing Bavarian culture and tradition.
