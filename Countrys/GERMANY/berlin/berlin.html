Berlin

Berlin, the capital of Germany, is known for its vibrant culture and rich history. As one of the most visited cities in Europe, Berlin offers something for everyone to enjoy. Visitors can explore famous landmarks such as the Brandenburg Gate and the Reichstag Building, Checkpoint Charlie and the Berlin Wall Memorial.

The city also boasts beautiful parks such as Tiergarten and Tempelhofer Feld, as well as world-renowned museums like the Pergamon Museum and the Jewish Museum. Berlin's culinary scene is diverse, with a wide variety of traditional German dishes and international cuisine.

Shopping in Berlin is a unique experience, with a wide range of independent boutiques and famous department stores such as KaDeWe and Galeries Lafayette. Berlin is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a mix of culture, history, entertainment, and the best of German art and architecture.

1. Brandenburg Gate

The Brandenburg Gate is one of the most iconic landmarks in Germany and a symbol of Berlin. It was built in the late 18th century and is located at the end of the famous Unter den Linden boulevard. The gate is a popular tourist attraction and has been featured in many films and TV shows. But did you know that it has some interesting facts that you might not have heard before?


  • The Brandenburg Gate was originally built as a symbol of peace between Prussia and France.

  • The gate has been damaged several times throughout its history, including during World War II.

  • The Brandenburg Gate is also home to the famous Quadriga sculpture, which depicts the goddess of victory riding a chariot pulled by four horses.

  • The gate has become an important symbol of German reunification since 1989.

2. Berlin Wall Memorial

The Berlin Wall Memorial is a memorial to the victims of the Berlin Wall and the division of Germany. It is located in the former no man's land between East and West Berlin, and consists of a preserved section of the wall, an open air exhibition, and a visitor center with a museum. The memorial was opened in 1998 and is now one of the most visited sites in Berlin.


  • The Berlin Wall Memorial is located at Bernauer Strasse, which was one of the most heavily fortified sections of the wall.

  • The memorial includes a preserved section of the wall, as well as an open air exhibition that tells the story of those who were affected by it.

  • The visitor center includes a museum with artifacts from both sides of the wall, including photos, documents, and personal items.

  • The memorial also includes an information center where visitors can learn more about life during this period in history.

3. Museum Island

Museum Island is a complex of five museums located on the northern part of an island in the Spree river in Berlin, Germany. The museums include the Altes Museum, Neues Museum, Alte Nationalgalerie, Bode Museum and Pergamonmuseum. The complex was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999 and is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Berlin.

4. Reichstag Building

The Reichstag Building is a historical edifice in Berlin, Germany, constructed to house the Imperial Diet (German: Reichstag), of the German Empire. It was opened in 1894 and housed the Diet until 1933, when it was severely damaged after being set on fire. After World War II, the building fell into disuse; the parliament of the German Democratic Republic (the Volkskammer) met in the Palast der Republik in East Berlin, while the parliament of the Federal Republic of Germany (the Bundestag) met in the Bundeshaus in Bonn.

5. Checkpoint Charlie

Checkpoint Charlie was the name given by the Western Allies to the best-known Berlin Wall crossing point between East Berlin and West Berlin during the Cold War (1947–1991). It is located in the Friedrichstadt neighborhood and was a symbol of the Cold War, representing the separation of East and West. The name "Checkpoint Charlie" came from the letter C in the NATO phonetic alphabet.

6. Alexanderplatz

Alexanderplatz is a large public square and transport hub in the central Mitte district of Berlin. The square is named after the Russian Tsar Alexander I and is often referred to simply as Alex, which also denotes the larger neighbourhood stretching from Mollstraße in the northeast to Spandauer Straße and the Rotes Rathaus in the southwest.

7. Berlin Cathedral

Berlin Cathedral is the short name for the Evangelical Supreme Parish and Collegiate Church in Berlin, Germany. It is located on Museum Island in the Mitte borough. The current building was finished in 1905 and is a main work of Historicist architecture of the "Kaiserzeit". The Berlin Cathedral has never been a cathedral in the actual sense of that term since it has never been the seat of a bishop.

8. Jewish Museum Berlin

The Jewish Museum Berlin is a museum in Berlin, Germany devoted to Jewish history, life and culture. It is located on Lindenstraße in the Kreuzberg district of Berlin. The museum was established in 1933 and was later reopened in 2001 following a major redesign by architect Daniel Libeskind. It contains an extensive collection of German Jewish history and culture.

Tiergarten Park is a large public park in the center of Berlin, Germany. It is located between the Brandenburg Gate and the Reichstag building. The park is popular with both locals and tourists, and it features a variety of attractions, including monuments, gardens, lakes, and forests.

10. East Side Gallery

The East Side Gallery is a 1.3 km long section of the Berlin Wall located near the center of Berlin, Germany. It is an open air gallery that features a collection of murals painted by artists from all over the world. The gallery serves as a memorial to freedom and serves as a reminder of the division between East and West Berlin during the Cold War.
